Why We're Here

Yahoo is evaluating Fin AI to transform customer support post-AOL separation - moving from BPO dependency and a rigid AI tool toward an AI-first platform that can resolve account recovery, handle FAQ volume, and scale without headcount. Four priorities are driving this.

🏢

Post-AOL separation demands a standalone platform

Yahoo separated from AOL and needs its own customer support infrastructure urgently. The current Salesforce instance is shared with AOL. An out-of-the-box solution like Intercom closes a multi-year gap in weeks, not years.

🔐

Account recovery is 90% of real support volume

Of ~200,000 real monthly queries (after filtering fraud and spam), the vast majority involve account access, identity recovery, and authentication. Yahoo needs authenticated, API-driven resolution flows - not FAQ bots.

💸

BPO costs need to scale down as AI scales up

700+ agents across 3 global BPOs (Romania, Philippines, Taiwan). Yahoo is financially controlled with close expense scrutiny. The goal isn't replacing BPO costs with equivalent new costs - it's eliminating them.

🔀

Sierra is too rigid for what comes next

Current AI tool works for basic FAQ but can't support Yahoo's vision: a single intelligent entry point where search routes to help articles or conversational AI, powering identity, mail, finance, and sports from one platform.

The Business Case

Current state: 800,000 monthly inbound contacts, with 75% classified as fraud, spam, or low-value - leaving ~200,000 real queries handled by 700+ agents across three global BPOs. Every real query touches a human today. Industry benchmarks for consumer-scale digital support put fully-loaded cost at $8-12 per resolved contact. At a conservative $8/contact, Yahoo's current model costs roughly $19M annually just in BPO labor for real query volume.

With Fin handling FAQ and account recovery: Account recovery and FAQ represent the vast majority of real query volume - highly repeatable, process-driven, and directly automatable with the right API integrations. At 5M annual Fin resolutions (a conservative starting point given Yahoo's scale), the majority of BPO contact volume is absorbed by Fin. Agent count drops from 700+ toward 400 by year-end as automation replaces routine work.

The cost of staying on Sierra

Sierra handles FAQ but doesn't touch account recovery - Yahoo's largest volume category. Every quarter on the current stack is another quarter of 200,000 monthly contacts routed to BPO agents at full cost, with no data on what could have been automated, no platform foundation for mail/finance/sports AI expansion, and no progress toward the agent reduction target. The AOL Salesforce separation also moves forward in parallel - without a clear helpdesk destination, that migration becomes more expensive, not less.

What We Keep Hearing

Account recovery is the problem Fin has to solve first

Vaishnavi confirmed identity issues are 90% of chatbot volume. Hacked accounts, forgotten passwords, email restrictions, MFA challenges - these are Yahoo's core support reality. Fin must handle authenticated recovery flows with API-driven actions, not just surface help articles.

One search bar. Not a chatbot and a search box.

Aladdin and Gary have been consistent: Yahoo's vision is a single intelligent entry point where a user types their problem and the system routes to a help article or AI conversational support seamlessly. The help center stays static for SEO. The AI sits on top. They don't want users choosing between tools.

The CMS complexity is real and cannot be underestimated

27 locales, two brands (Yahoo and Sky), locale-specific content differences driven by legal requirements - not just translation. Yahoo owns the front-end. Intercom is the back-end API. Jennifer and David's session confirmed this is a non-trivial build. Andy confirmed locale-specific content per brand is in development.

They want to own the platform without depending on Intercom to run it

Non-technical teams - PMs, content ops, support leads - must be able to configure AI logic, update flows, and publish content independently. No tickets to Intercom for changes. The exec pre-read framed this explicitly: Yahoo team owns the platform without relying on Intercom services.

The pricing model needs to align with the goal, not work against it

Delancey raised a clear concern: if Yahoo's goal is reducing contacts, a model that charges per resolution could misalign incentives. Volume-based conversational pricing was introduced as an alternative. This has been partially resolved but the commercial structure still needs final alignment.

This is a high-visibility internal program with financial scrutiny

Yahoo separated from AOL. This CX transformation is a high-visibility internal project with close expense scrutiny and a potential exit scenario in view. Every dollar needs clear justification. Gary has been advocating for time-to-market focus - a multi-year Salesforce migration alternative has already been rejected.
